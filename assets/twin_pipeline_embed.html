2a

2c

Embed → attention-weighted composite vector

Each zone sentence → one 1536-d vector (5 zones, 5 vectors). Fused by attention-weighted average (magnitude × context weight, softmax), then L2-normalized → one composite 1536-d vector = the twin for that week.

identityaccess_pattern data_behaviornetwork_footprint risk_posture

Weights uniform (~0.2 each) in normal ops; an insider investigation re-weights data_behavior→0.4, an APT hunt emphasizes network_footprint + risk_posture. Averaging (not concatenation) keeps the twin in the same 1536-d space as the threat concepts — which is what makes 2e possible.

2d

Trajectory & drift — per zone + composite

The twin is decomposed into the five zones, each tracked independently, so every zone yields its own drift. drift = 1 − cos(week t, week t+1), for each zone and the composite. CUSUM accumulates sub-threshold drift: cusum = max(0, cusum + drift − 0.02); fires at ≥ 0.05 for ≥ 2 weeks.

2e

Drift direction & MITRE projection

Replaces "87% anomalous" with a diagnosis. drift_vector = normalize(v_new − v_old), projected (cosine) onto 14 threat + 2 benign concepts embedded in the same space. Top concept a threat with alignment ≥ 0.15 → flag + emit MITRE techniques. Zone-specific filtering kills cross-domain false alignments.

identity STABLE (0.003), network_footprint DRIFTING (0.082) toward c2_beacon [T1071, T1573]

Two benign anchors (normal_role_change, seasonal_variation) sit alongside the threats — if drift aligns to a benign concept, the alert is suppressed. That is how false positives are damped.

Composite Scoring & Ranking

Five phases collapse into one comparable, explainable, ranked number.

FORMULA

Five phases, group-relative

Features → z-scores vs the entity's own role group (admin · security · developer · business · executive). An attacker must be abnormal for its own role — a far higher bar than a global threshold.

composite = signal_strength × 1.0 sum of top-3 feature z + breadth_15 × 0.5 # features z > 1.5 + sustained × 0.3 top-2 zone sustained z + max(ctx_spread_z,0) × 0.5 + max(ctx_max_z,0) × 0.3 + novelty × 1.0 C2 persistence → Stage 4

4

Novelty Persistence

The C2 catch. Works on the actual external IPs each entity contacts — not just the count.

METHOD

Per-entity novel-IP tracking

  • Baseline: first 10 weeks = the entity's normal external destinations.
  • From week 10 on, any external IP not in that baseline = NOVEL (judged per-entity).
  • max_persistence = most weeks any single novel IP recurs — the key C2 signal.
novelty = min(max_persistence / 5, 10) when persist > 10 wk + weeks_frac × 3 when frac > 0.5

A one-off new site appears once and vanishes. A C2 beacon is a novel IP the host calls back to week after week — that persistence is the signature.
